University of Oxford

Process Dynamics Lab

Welcome to our Oxford research group, where we combine materials science, manufacturing, and data analytics to understand how materials evolve during processing. Using advanced X-ray methods and AI, we aim to improve performance, optimise manufacturing, and develop low-emission materials for a sustainable, fusion-enabled future.

Dr. Enzo Liotti

Lecturer in Processing of Advanced Materials

​I earned my Bachelor’s (BSc 2004) and Master’s (MSc 2006) degrees in Material Engineering from Politecnico di Milano, Italy. Subsequently, I completed my PhD in Materials Science at the University of Loughborough in 2011, with a thesis focused on the characterization of nano-quasicrystalline aluminum alloys for high-temperature applications. The project involved the use of several synchrotron-based techniques, such as neutron and X-ray diffraction and X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XANES and EXAFS). My experience with synchrotron research inspired me to pursue an academic career, leading to a postdoctoral position at the University of Oxford's Department of Materials from 2011 to 2019, where I worked on in-situ imaging of solidification with Professor Patrick Grant. In 2019, I advanced to a Departmental Lecturer position in the same department.

Over the past 12 years, I have pioneered cutting-edge X-ray imaging techniques and data analysis algorithms to investigate critical physical phenomena during metal solidification. Throughout this period, detector technologies and X-ray sources have been continuously improving research capabilities, and capitalising on my multi-disciplinary research approach, divided equally among experimentation, technique development and data analytics, I have positioned myself at the forefront of these innovations, translating them into tools to extract scientific knowledge from observations.

I am principal investigator on two EPSRC funded projects (LiveX, £1.1m, EP/W024829/1; AIXISuMM, £2.7m, EP/X03884X/1) dedicated to the development of a new generation of multi-modal X-ray imaging techniques, named Artificial Intelligence X-ray Imaging (AIXI). I am also co-investigator, and Oxford lead, on the Materials 4.0 CDT (lead by Henry Royce Institute).

Dr Akash Aggarwal

I am currently a Postdoc, working on the project "Artificial Intelligence Supported X-ray Imaging for Sustainable Metal Manufacturing". Prior to this, I spent 2.5 years as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Empa – Swiss Federal Laboratories for Materials Science and Technology, where my work combined computational and experimental approaches. We developed coupled CFD-DEM models to investigate particle behaviour during Laser Directed Energy Deposition (LDED), performed in-situ synchrotron imaging of laser-induced powder stream dynamics, and contributed to the CALPHAD based alloy design of precious metals—including gold, silver, and platinum alloys in collaboration with industrial partners Richemont and Neoshapes. My PhD research, registered at IIT Kanpur and carried out at IIT Kanpur (India) and Purdue University (USA), focused on the investigation of dynamic laser beam interaction and melt pool evolution in laser-assisted metal additive manufacturing. My Master’s thesis at IIT Kanpur explored melting modes and keyhole instabilities in laser welding of Ti6Al4V, combining mesoscale thermo-fluidic modelling in OpenFOAM with experimental validation.

akash.aggarwal@materials.ox.ac.uk

Alessandro De Nardi

I am a DPhil student under the supervision of Dr. Enzo Liotti, Prof. Patrick Grant, and Prof. Roger Reed, focusing on the in situ real-time study of superalloys' solidification. I obtained my bachelor's degree at Università degli Studi di Trento and my master’s degree at Politecnico di Torino, where I was an active member of the Formula Student team. I conducted my master’s project at IMDEA Materials in Spain. Prior to starting my DPhil, I worked for an aerospace company specialising in Ni-superalloys.

alessandro.denardi@exeter.ox.ac.uk

Hiiro Moriyama

I am a DPhil student in the Department of Materials at the University of Oxford and a member of Cohort 2 of the Materials 4.0 CDT, led by the Henry Royce Institute. My research focuses on developing a multi-modal X-ray imaging technique that integrates diffraction, spectroscopy, and artificial intelligence to map hydrogen in metals and investigate its behaviour in transport, trapping, and embrittlement under in-situ conditions. My work is supervised by Dr Enzo Liotti.

Before commencing my DPhil, I worked at the Spanish National Research Council (CSIC) in Madrid and gained research experience at world-leading facilities, including the Stanford Linear Accelerator (SLAC), the German Electron Synchrotron (DESY), and the European XFEL. I have received several international awards, most notably the 2024 Golden Proton Award at an international science competition held at CERN.

hiiro.moriyama@materials.ox.ac.uk

Mehmet Emre Bozkurt

I am a DPhil student under the supervision of Dr Enzo Liotti, Professor Andrew Zisserman, and Sarp Ulas Ocak. My research focuses on Artificial Intelligence X-ray imaging. Specifically, we aim to leverage recent advancements in deep learning to develop multi-modal X-ray imaging models for applications in metal solidification processes and the extraction of information in real-time during experiments. I completed my BSc in Electrical and Electronics Engineering at Bilkent University. Before starting my DPhil, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer, specialising in computer vision tasks such as real-time object detection and tracking, SAR satellite image processing, and segmentation.

mehmet.bozkurt@worc.ox.ac.uk

Mingji Dai

I am a first year DPhil student in Dr Liotti's group.

I received my Bachelor degree from the Department of Materials, Nanjing University of Science and Technology, China, and my Master degree by research from the School of Physics, University of Bristol.

My DPhil project focusses on Artificial Intelligence in X-ray Imaging of Metal Solidification.

mingji.dai@materials.ox.ac.uk

Shun Yang

I am a DPhil student in the Group, under the supervision of Dr Enzo Liotti and Prof. Andrew Zisserman, focusing on Artificial Intelligence X-ray Imaging. My research involves applying computer vision and deep learning methods to process multi-category datasets of X-ray Images from metal solidification experiments. I completed my BEng degree in the Department of Materials at Jilin University and obtained my MS degree at Imperial College London.

shun.yang@materials.ox.ac.uk

Stefano Palazzo

I am a DPhil student in the Department of Materials, supervised by Dr. Enzo Liotti and Prof. Patrick Grant. My research focuses on the investigation of hot tearing mechanisms in recycled aluminium alloys using real-time, in-situ X-ray techniques. The project aims to advance the understanding of hot tears nucleation and propagation, a phenomenon particularly relevant to aluminium from recycled sources.

Before joining the Processing of Advanced Materials Group, I obtained a BSc in Materials Engineering and Nanotechnology from Politecnico di Milano and an MSc in Materials Science and Engineering from University College Dublin, where I was awarded the Medal for Academic Excellence for my MSc research project. After completing my master’s degree, I worked as a Materials Applications Engineer in the aerospace industry.

stefano.palazzo@materials.ox.ac.uk
